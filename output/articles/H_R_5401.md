# 9/11 Memorial and Museum Act

**Bill ID**: H.R.5401

**Sponsor**: D000632

**Committees**: hsii00

---

On September 12, 2023, Representative Anthony D'Esposito of New York's 4th district introduced H.R.5401, the 9/11 Memorial and Museum Act, in the House. The bill directs the Department of Homeland Security to award a one-time grant to the nonprofit organization operating the National September 11 Memorial & Museum in New York City for the operation, security, and maintenance of these facilities. As a condition for receiving the grant, the organization must ensure free admission to all associated memorial and museum facilities for active and retired military personnel.

The bill has moved through several stages within the House. After referral to the House Committee on Natural Resources, it underwent committee consideration and markup sessions. On November 20, 2023, the bill was ordered to be reported in the nature of a substitute by unanimous consent. It then passed the House under suspension of the rules with a voice vote on December 11, 2023, after forty minutes of debate.

The sponsor of H.R.5401 is Rep. Anthony D'Esposito [Rep. D'Esposito, Anthony](https://www.congress.gov/member/D000632), a Republican representing New York's 4th district. As a member of the House Committee on Natural Resources, D'Esposito has been instrumental in advancing this legislation.
