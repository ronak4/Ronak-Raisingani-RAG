# Stop EV Freeloading Act

**Bill ID**: S.2882

**Sponsor**: F000463

**Committees**: ssfi00

---

On September 21, 2023, Senator Deb Fischer from Nebraska introduced the Stop EV Freeloading Act (S.2882) in the Senate. The bill aims to impose new excise taxes on electric vehicle sales and battery modules, with the collected funds directed towards the Highway Trust Fund for transportation-related projects.

The proposed legislation would levy a $1,000 tax on each electric vehicle sold and a $550 tax on each battery module weighing over 1,000 pounds used in an electric vehicle. The bill defines an electric vehicle as a light-duty motor vehicle with a gross weight of less than 8,500 pounds that is capable of being propelled by an electric motor. These new taxes are intended to generate additional revenue for infrastructure projects and address what some lawmakers perceive as the "freeloading" issue faced by traditional fuel-based vehicles.

The bill was referred to the Senate Committee on Finance, where it will undergo further scrutiny and potential amendments before any votes can be taken. As of now, no specific actions or hearings have been scheduled. The committee plays a crucial role in shaping legislation related to taxation and trade policy, making its review process essential for the bill's progress.

Senator Deb Fischer is the primary sponsor of S.2882, representing Nebraska as a Republican. She has been a vocal advocate for addressing infrastructure funding challenges through innovative means such as this proposed excise tax on electric vehicles. The bill also has three cosponsors: Senator Pete Ricketts from Nebraska, Senator John Cornyn from Texas, and Senator Cynthia M. Lummis from Wyoming.
