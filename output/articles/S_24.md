On January 23, 2023, Senator Dianne Feinstein (D-CA) introduced the "Fighting Homelessness Through Services and Housing Act" (S.24), a bill aimed at addressing homelessness by providing grants to local and tribal governments for planning and implementing programs related to housing and services for homeless individuals and families. Currently in its initial stages of the legislative process, S.24 is awaiting consideration by the Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions.

The bill requires the Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) to award grants over a five-year period to assist local and tribal governments in carrying out activities and covering capital building costs associated with providing housing and services for homeless individuals. This comprehensive approach seeks to ensure that communities have the resources necessary to address homelessness comprehensively, from planning stages through implementation.

The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee plays a crucial role in this process. As the primary committee responsible for reviewing legislation related to health care, education, labor, and pensions, it will assess the bill's merits and potential impact on these areas. The committee may hold hearings, consider amendments, and ultimately decide whether to advance the bill or send it back with modifications.

Senator Dianne Feinstein (D-CA) is the sponsor of S.24.
